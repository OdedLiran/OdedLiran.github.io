On-going projects

1. Remote measurement of Crop Treatment Adequacy

Agricultural technological innovations are nowadays much needed in Northern Israel, particularly due to the reduced supply of fresh water for farming, with the intent to minimize the eutrophication of the Sea of Galilee. The implementation of Remote Sensing techniques within commercial products has the potential to provide the required solution of inputs minimization and therefore increase in revenues. Remote Sensing of vegetation characteristics is used to track changes in biomass primary production on a global scale. While the long-term of this study is to understand what photosynthetic factors decouple the fluorescence yield from the actual primary production rate of crops, our immediate aim is to implement this technique in practical solutions. The targeted impact is to optimize the fertilization and irrigation treatments administered to the crops and by that lower the cost of inputs to the farmer.

2. Development of a precise irrigation control based on hyperspectral remote sensing of crop status

Considering that 60% of the world’s fresh water is used for irrigation, there is an unmet need for implementation of better and sustainable irrigation schemes. As of today, remote sensing technology is based on thermal imagery or multispectral imaging, both techniques are limited in their detection accuracy. This study offers an interdisciplinary approach combining better-resolved sensors, plant physical characteristics and machine learning algorithms. We develop a computer-controlled feedback loop that will assess the water content of crops and tune precise irrigation. Results of the project will yield an artificial intelligence engine with the sole purpose to irrigate correctly and responsibly each part within the field, thus providing a substantial competition in the multimillion dollars market of precision irrigation.
