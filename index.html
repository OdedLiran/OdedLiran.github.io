Welcome to the website of the group of Agrophysics studies.

Modern agriculture faces an ultimatum – to feed the ever growing world population which is predicted to bypass the 9 billion planet habitats mark by the year of 2050 and by that pass the amount of food it can supply. As rulers of the top of the food chain, crops perform photosynthesis and provide us not only with food but also with fibers and fuel, rendering photosynthesis as the single most important mechanism that is beneficial to understand and explore. The research led by the group is of an applicative nature, where we implement our academic knowledge in smart and innovative technologies that monitor the well-being of the crops. We hope that you will find the information provided within this website useful and interesting as we are always looking for collaborations- be it scientific or industrial.

Research Interests

Our laboratory main goal is to understand how plants utilize sunlight energy in order to produce biomass. We therefore research photosynthesis physiology at the organism’s level. As we are situated in an agriculture research institute we ask questions related mostly to how crops experience their environment when exposed to biotic and abiotic stresses. To do so, we use and develop working protocols which measure the plants’ photosynthetic apparatus from single leaf to canopies and whole field level while using both proximal and remote sensing measurement techniques on our journey. In order to find interesting patterns in the enormous amount of data collected on the way, as experts in statistics, we use data science to analyze and classify our findings.
